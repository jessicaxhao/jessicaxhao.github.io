About Us

The following are brief biographies of the couple written by their respective other halves. We hope you enjoy reading these as much as we enjoyed writing them.

Jessica Hao Henry Chai

Jessica Hao

You would never hear her say it but Jessica Meng Jia Hao is a straight-up badass. Her utter bad-assery can be seen in many aspects of her life, from how she eats a dozen raw eggs (blended thoroughly with whey protein of course) for breakfast to how she brawls at the end of her beloved Chicago Blackhawks’ hockey games, regardless of whether or not they won. She excels at not taking any bullshit from anyone, putting uppity and self-righteous people in their place, and pushing herself to her limits in everything that she does, whether it’s school, working out or slaughtering her enemies.

Jessica was born in Beijing, China and moved to Ontario, Canada when she was six. Her family lived there until she was 13, when they moved back to China, this time to Shanghai. In that time, her younger brother, Coen, was born and rapidly proved himself to be the more academically competent of the two children, racing past his older sister in reading level and mathematical aptitude.

As a small she-child, Jess always looked longingly across the Ambassador Bridge, the bridge that connected her hometown of Windsor to Detroit, at the United States of America, the place that she loves with all of her heart. America, with all of its liberty, truly conservative politics and none of those pesky taxes or public services like free universal healthcare (ewww), was truly where she longed to be.

However, it was not until she graduated from the Shanghai American School (a subconscious nod to her love of her soon to be adopted country?) that she was able to achieve her dream of living in America. She attended “just another school in New Jersey” where she studied “not a real science” on her way to becoming a doctor. She worked very hard, rarely partying or interacting with boys because, let’s face it, boys are gross. She now attends medical school at Washington University, with the long-term goal of being a stay-at-home trophy mom.

Some fun facts about Jess that are sure to be great conversation starters when you see her at the wedding include:

  • Jess loves talking about cars, particularly foreign made, practical and economically-sensible sedans; she dreams about one day owning a responsible and fuel-efficient Toyota Camry.
  • History has always been one of Jess’s favorite subjects; she definitely knows when World War II occurred and can definitely tell you who fought whom in said war.
  • Country music is what lives in Jess’s soul; some of her favorite tracks include “I lost my tractor and now my heart is broken” and “My girl’s been sleeping with my horses and that’s okay”.

Henry Chai

Henry R. Chai is a sort of okay dude from basically Canada whose greatest life accomplishment thus far has been getting engaged to Jess. Henry grew up in the kind of small town that causes every kid who grows up there to write at least one song revolving around the themes of “being trapped in this awful place” and “needing to escape”. The status of the synthesizer that sits still preserved in his childhood bedroom suggests that Henry was no exception to this rule. This small town gave Henry a false sense of his value and worth in the world. His childhood successes in chess club, MathCounts, and the All-State orchestra led him to believe that he would be qualified to apply to the best university in the country. Luckily, a subsequent clerical error resulted in his acceptance.

As a result, Henry graduated as a member of Princeton's Great Class of 2014. During his time on campus, he was known for his indecisiveness regarding his future major, his role in providing Dan Marks with the largest single on campus, and the fact that he had hands down the best Beirut partner in the universe. He currently lives in Boston, MA where he juggles his two full-time jobs as code-running slave at a boutique operations consulting firm and professional survivor of terrible soul-sucking New England winters.

In his free time, Henry likes to talk about not talking about Eli Manning and denying the fact that he spends a substantive amount of his life singing the “Nationwide is on your side” song in his head in the other Manning’s voice. He generally partakes in these activities while participating in no less than 12 fantasy sports leagues at all times and losing in most of them every week by doing things like not starting Matthew Stafford on days when he goes for 30+ points. Henry’s incredible memory for sports-related statistics allows him to relive every one of the crushing defeats he brings upon himself week after week in vivid detail when he is not actively in the midst of compulsively watching RedZone.

Henry has a storied history in the world of video gaming. His glory days peaked during his college years of Tetris Friends but an aging mind and rapidly deteriorating reflexes required that he switch to a less fast-paced game more suitable to his present disabilities. Henry has since then made a small dent in the world of competitive Hearthstone, where he has posted a best finish of Rank 3 on the Ladder, exactly 3 ranks short of anything notable.

Outside of his E-sports pursuits, Henry is also an active role-player in a local Dungeons and Dragons group where he gets to live out his fantasies of being a monk with really great cooking abilities. DND allows Henry the opportunity to be someone he would otherwise never have the ability to be in the real world, like an individual with an actual moral compass, life goals, and self-sufficiency.

Max Wang - Man of Honour

Facebook recently alerted me that I have been friends with Jess for over 8 years. Daisy Lopez - Bridesmaid
